### Source: SOIC (School of Intrinsic Compounding) — "When to Buy a Stock" Webinars (Parts 1 & 2)

## Overview: The Elephant Story — Five Lenses

Like blind men feeling different parts of an elephant, each investing approach captures part of the truth:
1. **Technicals** — price and volume action
2. **Value Buying** — fundamental undervaluation
3. **Momentum Buying** — earnings and price acceleration
4. **Techno-Funda** — fundamentals + technical confirmation
5. **CANSLIM** — systematic growth + momentum framework

The best investors combine multiple lenses rather than dogmatically following one approach.

---

## PART A: FUNDAMENTAL REASONS TO BUY

### 1. Bombed Out IPOs

**5-Step IPO Journey:**
```
Pre-IPO window dressing → Elevated base → Benign base → Earnings surprise → IPO base breakout
```

**Three Entry Strategies:**
1. **Trend Change** — when price action turns positive after extended decline
2. **Improved Results** — when earnings begin recovering from post-IPO disappointment
3. **IPO Base Breakout** — technical breakout after basing phase

**Case Studies:** PB Fintech, Delhivery, Mrs Bectors Food

**Why it works:** IPOs often disappoint in Year 1-2 as window-dressing unwinds. Patience until the real business emerges creates excellent entry points.

---

### 2. Inflection Plays

Upgrade cycles last several quarters; downgrade cycles too. Buy at the inflection point of a multi-quarter upgrade cycle.

**8-Point Checklist to Spot Inflection:**
1. Margin guidance turning positive
2. Quarterly numbers showing sequential improvement
3. Capacity utilisation rising
4. Order book growing
5. Margin expansion becoming visible
6. Massive volumes (institutional interest beginning)
7. Trend change in price action
8. Market share being gained

**Case Studies:**
- **TVS Motors** — market share from 14% to 20%; inflection visible in margin guidance
- **Aarti Industries** — chemicals downcycle bottom; inflection in order book
- **Apar Industries** — industrial capex cycle beginning
- **Acutaas Chemicals** — product mix shift inflection

---

### 3. Speed Breaker Approach

Buy on sudden, short-lived negative events that create extreme dislocation in quality businesses.

**Types of Speed Breakers:**
- IT raids / regulatory notices
- Regulatory price caps
- CEO resignation
- Tariff uncertainty / macro noise
- Software/operational glitch

**Critical Test (before buying):**
> Is the issue short-lived and materialistically small? OR is it a long-term structural threat?

**Pros:** Huge valuation discounts; high near-term IRR
**Cons:** Could be permanent (Delta Corp, IEX — structural threats not speed breakers)

**Case Studies:**
| Company | Speed Breaker | Outcome |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| Polycab | IT raid | Speed breaker — bought, recovered strongly |
| MCX | Software issue | Speed breaker — temporary disruption |
| Infosys | Vishal Sikka resignation | Speed breaker — management continuity maintained |
| Hospital sector | Price capping | Speed breaker — caps were partial and temporary |
| Multiple companies | Trump tariffs | Depends on specific exposure |

---

### 4. Pessimism Plays

Buy what everyone hates at 52-week lows or multi-year consolidation.

**Characteristics:**
- Everyone hates the sector/company
- Growth missing or negative
- Requires patience (12-24+ months)
- Stocks at 52-week lows or multi-year consolidation bases

**Pros:** Benign valuations; can make outsized returns when cycle turns
**Risk:** Bad cycle persists longer than expected

**Case Studies:** Microfinance sector at cycle bottom; Dairy (seasonal pessimism); Cement (election-led soft demand)

---

### 5. Value Investing — Six Types

**Type 1: Below Book Value**
- Buy quality businesses when trading below book value
- Example: JM Finance at <1x P/B during market stress

**Type 2: Cyclicals at Bottom of Cycle**
- Buy when EV/EBITDA or EV/replacement cost is very low
- Example: HLE Glasscoat at cycle bottom

**Type 3: Buy Before Cycle Turns**
- Identify turning point signals before earnings recover
- Examples: Mold-Tek Packaging, Pesticide companies

**Type 4: PEG < 1x**
- Growth companies where PE is less than growth rate
- Examples: Indian Shipbuilders during order cycle beginning

**Type 5: Bottom Margins**
- Buy when margins are at historical lows and are mean-reverting
- Example: Deepak Fertilisers at margin trough

**Type 6: High Dividend Yield**
- PSUs / commodity companies with high yield acting as floor
- Example: Coal India + PSU pack

**Critical Rule:** Value plays **must have a catalyst**. Always add in tranches.

**Twin Engine Returns:** Buying at bottom gives:
1. Earnings acceleration (as cycle recovers)
2. PE re-rating (as market recognises the recovery)

**Example:** Newgen Software — bought at bottom; both engines fired simultaneously → exceptional returns

**Value Traps to Avoid:** Karnataka Bank; Gas distribution (IGL/MGL in structural decline)

---

### 6. Pre-Opex Phase

**4-Phase Capex Cycle:**
```
Capex → Pre-Opex (margins fall, D&A rises) → Commercialisation → Operating Leverage
```

**How to Spot:** Capex announcement followed by increase in depreciation without corresponding revenue increase.

**Why buy:** Market punishes the margin dip; the recovery is sharp when utilisation ramps.

**Case Study:** Concord Biotech — injectable facility caused EBITDA margins to fall from 37.7% to 30.1%. Knowing the pre-opex cycle, this was a buy, not a sell.

---

### 7. Order Book / Fundamental Buy Point

Enter before order book inflection shows up in financials; ride until peak profitability with no incremental order visibility.

**Case Studies:** GMM Pfaudler (reactor order book), Tejas Networks (telecom capex cycle)

## PART B: SOIC UNIQUE BUYING PATTERNS

### 1. Convexity Plays

**Concept (Taleb's Antifragile):** Limited downside + non-linear upside

Structure to look for:
- Baseline business protecting downside
- Optionality from new project / expansion with massive upside potential
- Market not pricing in the optionality

**Case Studies:**
- **APL Apollo Vision 2020 (2017-19)** — established base + structural growth optionality
- **Godawari Power** — massive multi-project capex; each successful creates exponential returns
- **Sarda Energy** — SKS Power acquisition; acquisition optionality at negligible cost to base

---

### 2. Structural Mix Change ("Boiling Frog" Model)

Small, gradual changes in business mix → non-linear outcome over 3-5 years.

**What to Track:**
- Is the new segment higher value-addition?
- Does it address a larger TAM?
- Is it more scalable?
- Is the mix shift accelerating or decelerating?

**Case Studies:**
| Company | Mix Change | Outcome |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Neuland Labs | API → CDMO; CMS mix 15% → 50% | Margin expansion + re-rating |
| Garware Hi-Tech | Commodity polyester → specialty VAP | EBITDA margin 9% → 23.5% |
| Aarti Pharmalabs | CDMO growing to Rs.800-1000 Cr business | Structural re-rating |

---

### 3. Special Situations

**Types:**
1. **Demergers** — Edelweiss/EAAA; unlocking hidden value
2. **Reverse Mergers** — HLF/NDL Ventures
3. **M&A** — JSW+AkzoNobel, Torrent+JB Chemicals
4. **Failed situations** — Vedanta (risk of poor capital allocation)

**Risks:** Valuation overhang during transition; integration risk in M&A

### 4. Self-Help Stories

**Three Pillars of Self-Help:**
1. Improving corporate structure (simplification, deleveraging)
2. Focus on core operations (exit non-core)
3. Exit low-value businesses

**Case Studies:**
| Company | Self-Help Action | Outcome |
|---------|-----------------|---------|
| Jayswal Neco | Debt refinancing | Cost structure improvement |
| Pennar Industries | Margin mix shift | Revenue quality improvement |
| Centum Electronics | Subsidiary exit | Focus + margin expansion |
| Arvind Fashions | Brand/channel focus | Working capital improvement |

---

### 5. Momentum Investing

Not just technical momentum — find "purple patch" companies in hyper earnings growth.

**Key Rules:**
- Hyper earnings growth is unsustainable long-term — know when to exit
- Track whether growth rate is accelerating or decelerating

**Case Studies:**
- **Frontier Springs** — identified early in purple patch
- **Dixon Tech** — 50.2% PAT CAGR over 10 years; exceptional momentum
- **Trent** — earnings momentum slowing → exit signal

Signs that institutions are quietly accumulating:
1. Volume above average on up days
2. Relative strength vs market improving
3. Tight price ranges (low volatility while price consolidates)
4. Higher lows forming
5. Sector peers also showing similar patterns
6. Price holding key moving averages on dips
7. Decreasing volume on pullbacks
8. Expanding volume on breakouts
9. Stock performing better than sector on bad market days
10. Insider buying / institutional disclosures

## BUY DECISION CHECKLIST

### Fundamental Checklist
- [ ] What is the buy thesis? Can I state it in 2-3 sentences?
- [ ] Which of the 7 fundamental buy patterns does this fit?
- [ ] What is the catalyst? When does it play out?
- [ ] What is the downside if I am wrong?
- [ ] Is the business in a purple patch or entering one?
- [ ] Is this a speed breaker (temporary) or structural decline?
- [ ] What is the valuation? Am I paying for potential or confirmed results?
- [ ] Is this a value trap (low price but no catalyst) or genuine value?

### Technical / Timing Checklist
- [ ] What stage is the stock in? (Stage 1/2/3/4)
- [ ] Is the market in a bull, bear, or sideways phase?
- [ ] Is relative strength improving vs sector and market?
- [ ] Is volume supportive (high on up days, low on down days)?
- [ ] Are there footprints of smart money accumulation?
- [ ] Is there a clean base or pattern to buy off?

### Position Sizing & Entry
- [ ] How should I size the initial position (high vs medium vs low conviction)?
- [ ] Should I enter in one tranche or multiple tranches?
- [ ] What would make me add to the position?
- [ ] What would make me sell the position?
- [ ] Have I defined the stop-loss or exit criteria before entry?

## KEY MENTAL MODELS

**1. Twin Engine Returns:** At a bottom, both earnings recovery AND PE re-rating happen simultaneously → biggest returns come from buying when everyone is pessimistic.

**2. Upgrade vs Downgrade Cycles:** These last multiple quarters. Identify early in the cycle; the market is slow to price in multi-quarter upgrades.

**3. Speed Breaker vs Structural Decline:** Always ask — is this problem temporary or permanent? The best returns come from correctly identifying speed breakers that the market treats as structural.

**4. Conviction = Position Size:** High-conviction ideas deserve larger positions. Do not over-diversify. Quality of position matters more than quantity.

**5. Purple Patch Exit:** Every purple patch ends. The exit signal is deceleration in earnings growth rate — not absolute earnings, but the rate of change.

**6. Pre-Opex is a Feature, Not a Bug:** Companies investing in growth will show margin compression in the pre-opex phase. This is a buy signal for quality businesses, not a sell signal.

## 1. BOMBED-OUT IPOs

**5-Stage IPO Journey:**
1. Pre-IPO Window Dressing — Companies inflate margins before IPO
2. Elevated Base — Initial growth struggles due to inflated numbers
3. Benign Base — Operations normalize
4. Surprise in Earnings Growth — Improved fundamentals → positive surprises
5. IPO Base Breakout — Sustained performance, stock reclaims IPO highs

**Three Entry Methods:**
1. **Trend Change** — Buy when lower highs → higher highs; rounding bottom. Examples: Policybazaar, Zomato
2. **Improved Financial Results** — Buy when results/guidance improve. Example: Delhivery (from FY24)
3. **IPO Base Breakout** — Buy when stock breaks out of IPO base (exhausted forced sellers). Example: Mrs. Bectors Food (Rs 250 → Rs 1,600)

## 2. INFLECTION POINT BUYING

Upgrade cycles last several quarters; downgrade cycles also last several quarters.

**Case Study: TVS Motors**
- Market share: 14.04% (May 2021) → ~19.98% (July 2025) while Hero fell 46% → 25%
- Sales growth inflection post FY21 + EBITDA margin expansion 11% → 15%
- Relative Strength vs Nifty 52 confirmed the upgrade cycle

**Spotting Inflections — 8-Point Checklist:**
1. Guidance of margin improvement
2. Strong quarterly numbers or export data
3. Capacity utilisation improving post capex
4. Order book getting built
5. Margin expansion beginning
6. Massive volumes on chart
7. Long base breakout or primary trend change
8. Market share gain

**Case Study: Rounding Bottom**
JM Financial: Fundamentally at great value zone; technically forming rounding bottom, stopped making lower lows; RS vs Nifty 52 confirming turnaround.

## 3. SPEED BREAKER APPROACH

Buy during sudden extreme uncertainty events that cause a free fall despite intact business.

**Types of Speed Breakers:**
- GST / IT notices
- Regulatory issues (resolvable)
- Key person resignation
- Covid one-off
- Tariff uncertainty
- Industry-level noise

**Critical Judgment:** Is the issue short-lived and materialistically small? OR a serious long-term challenge?

**Case Studies:**

| Company | Speed Breaker | Verdict |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| Polycab | IT raid (50+ locations) | Speed breaker — worst case <5% of balance sheet |
| MCX | Software migration (63 Moons → TCS) | Resolvable operational issue |
| Infosys | Vishal Sikka resignation (2017) | Governance noise; long-term business intact |
| Hospital sector | SC price capping hearing | Materialistically impossible to implement |
| Delta Corp | Regulatory crackdown on online gaming | **Structural threat — NOT a speed breaker** |
| IEX | Market structure changes | **Structural threat — NOT a speed breaker** |

**Pros:** Quality companies at major valuation discounts; near-term IRR very high
**Cons:** Some setbacks ARE permanent (classify correctly)
